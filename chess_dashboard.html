♟️ Endgame Concepts

King + Pawn vs King — The Opposition

When only kings and pawns are left, your king must lead the pawn. "Opposition" means the two kings face each other with one square between them, and it's the opponent's turn — forcing them to step aside.

Example: White pawn on e5, White king on e4, Black king on e6. White plays Ke5 — Black must move aside (Kd6 or Kf6), and the pawn advances. Without this, even a winning pawn position can draw.

Practice: Lichess → Learn → Endgames → "King and Pawn"

Rook Behind the Passed Pawn

A passed pawn has no enemy pawn blocking it or on adjacent files — it can potentially run to promotion.

Your passed pawn: Put your rook behind it. As the pawn marches from e5 → e6 → e7, your rook's scope grows. Rook on e1, pawn on e5 → pawn advances, rook follows and gains power.

Opponent's passed pawn: Put your rook in front of it to block. Rook on e8 stops a pawn on e5 cold.

This came up in your losses vs gaindmaster and takesandthentakes.
